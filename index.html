Bio

Since 2008, I've lived in Washington, DC, where I work as a journalist for several media outlets. I cover the South Caucasus, Eastern Europe and Turkey. My areas of interest also include Central Asia politics, Turkish foreign policy, the Kurdish issue, and the Middle East. My articles have appeared in the:

  • Southeastern European Times,
  • Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty,
  • Foreign Policy Magazine, etc.

From 1999 to 2008, I covered Eurasian politics and security from Ankara, Baku, and Tbilisi, such as the Rose Revolution in Georgia, the Orange Revolution in Ukraine, the Georgian-Russian war, and Turkish-Armenian rapprochement.
